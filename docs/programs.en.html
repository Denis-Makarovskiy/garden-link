Programs · four directions
Executive Recovery
Burnout & overload

When it is needed:

  • Chronic fatigue that does not pass after rest
  • Reduced concentration and productivity
  • Sleep disturbances
  • Emotional burnout
  • Preparation for important negotiations, talks or events
  • Prolonged mental or physical overstrain
  • Comprehensive recovery with cardiovascular conditions (CHD, chronic heart failure, etc.)

Program goals: comprehensive assessment of physical and psycho‑emotional state; stabilization of the nervous system; higher concentration, clarity and capacity for work; lower anxiety and inner tension; better sleep; more energy and overall resource; restoration of natural energy mechanisms; cardiovascular support; better cellular metabolism and activation of mitochondrial energy production.

Body Balance Reset
Metabolism & self‑regulation

When it is needed:

  • Episodes of emotional overeating or disordered eating
  • Reduced appetite or loss of appetite
  • Irregular or excessively controlled eating
  • Long periods of dietary restriction
  • Chronic fatigue and low energy
  • Anxiety and emotional tension; sleep disturbances
  • Recovery after prolonged stress; signs of metabolic exhaustion

Program goals: assessment of physical and psycho‑emotional state; gentle regulation of metabolism; restoration of the nervous system; normalization of energy exchange; support of detoxification and liver function; lower anxiety and tension; restoration of eating behavior.

Post‑Surgery Recovery
Post‑operative rehabilitation

When it is needed:

  • Recovery period after surgery (once the acute stage is over)
  • General loss of energy and weakness after an operation
  • Swelling and slowed tissue recovery
  • Increased fatigue and reduced physical activity
  • Need for gentle recovery under medical supervision
  • Recovery after medication load; return to the usual level of activity

Program goals: assessment of physical and psycho‑emotional state; faster tissue recovery and regeneration; lower residual load on the body after surgery; liver support after drug therapy; reduced swelling and restored fluid balance; normalization of energy exchange; gentle return to physical and social activity.

The program is carried out only with the attending physician's approval. It is not used in the early post‑operative period.

Human Reserve
Sustaining resource

When it is needed:

  • Need to support immunity and antioxidant defense
  • High physical or mental loads
  • Recovery after intensive training
  • Wish to maintain energy, concentration and overall resource
  • Preventive health support and slowing of exhaustion

Program goals: assessment of physical and psycho‑emotional state; replenishment of vitamin, mineral and electrolyte deficiencies; sustaining and boosting the body's energy potential; strengthening the immune system; better sleep and lower stress; improved cognitive function; antioxidant defense and metabolic support; support of the cardiovascular and muscular systems.

Post‑program · Continuum

Supportive accompaniment

The Garden Recovery Continuum track helps preserve achieved results, stay in touch with specialists and hold the positive dynamics after the main program. Cost is calculated individually depending on the chosen format and intensity of support.

  • Integration Support · 30 days — for guests of Fast and Basic Recovery: 4 online sessions of 50 min, 1 per week, with the same specialist who worked with the guest.
  • Integration Support · 90 days — long‑term accompaniment: 12 online sessions of 50 min over 3 months; gradual integration of new habits into everyday life.
  • Maintenance track — 2 sessions per month to sustain a stable state and preventive support.
  • Therapeutic track — 4 sessions per month; work with persistent emotional and behavioral patterns.
  • Intensive track — 6–8 sessions per month; active transformational work and in‑depth therapeutic support.
  • Annual Retreat — a return visit in 6–12 months: repeat testing and diagnostics, updated medical and therapeutic tracks, adjustment of the personal plan, participation in the Garden Recovery loyalty program.
